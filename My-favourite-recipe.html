Tabbouleh Salad

Quick. Healthy. Delicious.

Ingredients

Instructions
  1. Pour the boiling water over the bulghur wheat in a shallow bowl. Use a fork to quickly stir the water into the wheat, then set aside for 10 minutes.
  2. Fluff the wheat with a fork, then place a plate (or plastic wrap) over the top of the bowl and place it in the fridge.
  3. Let rest until room temperature, at least 15 minutes.
  1. Wash the leaves of the parsley and mint then dry completely with a paper towel.
  2. Trim the stems and discard.
  3. Mince the parsley and mint leaves and place in a large bowl.
  1. Remove the cooled bulghur from the fridge and combine with the herbs, fluffing and tossing with a fork. Fold in the tomatoes, cucumber, and green onions.
  2. Drizzle in the olive oil and lemon juice and toss well. Sprinkle in the salt, stir, and taste. Add more salt if needed.
  3. Serve cool or at room temperature.

Fun facts:

Originally from the mountains of Lebanon and Syria, tabbouleh has become one of the most popular salads in the Middle East. In Lebanon, Syria, Palestine, and Jordan, the wheat variety salamouni cultivated in the region around Mount Lebanon, Beqaa Valley and Baalbek was considered (in the mid-19th century) as particularly well-suited for making bulgur, a basic ingredient of tabbouleh. Tabbouleh and other vegetable-based mezze dishes popular in Syria were mocked by Baghdadi women and cooks when they were first introduced to them, because they were seen as being a means to scrimp on the use of meat.
